- The goal of this evaluation was to compare two strategies for improving tax compliance among clients of high-risk tax preparers. The first was sending warning letters to preparers' clients and the second was conducting outreach directly to preparers. We had two key motivating questions. First, we wanted to learn whether sending letters to some clients of a tax preparer could affect the behavior of the preparer's other clients. If so, the IRS could achieve meaningful compliance gains while mailing fewer letters, thus reducing program costs. Second, we wanted to understand who should receive outreach to have the biggest impacts on tax return accuracy and potential cost savings: preparers, or preparers and their clients simultaneously.
