Overview of programs and evidence

The evaluation team collected information to describe program planning, implementation, data availability, and existing evaluations for the 32 programs that were highlighted in the Advancing Equity Through the ARP report. They did so through three activities—consultations, a review of documents, and a review of measures and data sources.

Specifically, this scan captured available information about programs:

  • Reach to the intended population
  • Effectiveness or efficacy
  • Adoption by intended staff, settings, systems, and communities
  • Implementation status, consistency, costs, adaptations made during delivery, and to what extent the program was implemented as intended
  • Maintenance/sustainment of intervention effects on individuals and settings across time

To guide the federal government’s exploration of questions about equitable recovery, the White House released the ARP Equity Learning Agenda, as part of the Advancing Equity Through the ARP report. The Equity Learning Agenda is designed to provide a framework for ensuring the federal government builds evidence about what works and what does not, to deliver an equitable recovery both now and in the future.
