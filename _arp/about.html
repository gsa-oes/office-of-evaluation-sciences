About the ARP National Evaluation

The goal of the national evaluation is to look systematically at the 32 programs included in the Advancing Equity Through the ARP report and provide an integrated account of implementation strategies. The American Rescue Plan included a complex array of programs led by different agencies and conducted in different ways. Many of these programs used similar strategies, focused on the same pressing concerns, and aimed to serve the same communities. This novel evaluation looks across these programs with shared approaches or goals to generate lessons that can inform program design and delivery across the Federal Government.

The ARP National Evaluation addresses two overarching research objectives that each include multiple questions:

Research question Methodology

RQ 1. Describe ARP program planning, implementation, and evaluation, including the integration of equity across these areas.

  • How were ARP-funded programs implemented at the federal and/or state level? What were the intended outcomes of these programs?
  • What approaches to stakeholder engagement did federal agencies use to inform program implementation?
  • How, and to what extent, did available evidence inform program design?
  • To what extent, and through what mechanisms, was an emphasis on improving equity integrated into ARP program implementation?
  • What data collection protocols were established to support monitoring, performance measurement, and evaluation?
  • To what extent are data available and of sufficient quality to assess the contribution of ARP investments and policy?
Environmental scan of 32 programs

RQ 2. Analyze the effectiveness of ARP program implementation, particularly with respect to advancing equity

  • To what extent did ARP investments and policy interventions advance equitable outcomes for those they were designed to serve?
  • What strategies contributed to the successes, and where are different strategies needed?
  • Where multiple ARP programs aim to improve similar outcomes, especially among a shared population:
    • To what extent is there coordination across programs in their administration, customer experience strategies, or performance or outcome measurement practices?
    • To what extent are there collective impacts that could be attributed to more than one program? What kinds of impacts, if any, are observed?
    • What kinds of secondary effects are observed that may not be captured in targeted outcome measures?

The multi-year evaluation is being conducted by the American Institutes for Research (AIR), with Abt Associates and Decision Information Resources. OES is overseeing the contract in partnership with the Office of Management and Budgetâ€™s Evidence Team, with support from agency partners.

An Agency Expert Team of agency staff with evaluation expertise, contract experience, and/or experience working on evaluations of programs relevant to American Rescue Plan (ARP) efforts provides input into the design and outputs of the evaluation. It currently includes representatives from the U.S. Department of Agriculture, Department of Education, Department of Health and Human Services, Department of Housing and Urban Development, Department of the Treasury, General Services Administration, and Small Business Administration.
