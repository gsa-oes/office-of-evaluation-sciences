## Background
The COVID-19 pandemic delivered a sudden and vast blow to the American publicsociety, the U.S. economy, and health outcomes, and the provision of healthcare. The federalU.S. government responded with historic investments includinglike the Coronavirus Aid, Relief, and Economic Security Act (CARES) of 2020 and the American Rescue Plan Act (ARP) of 2021, which were designed to help everyone, including those hardest hit. These significant investments begged the question:

Which programs and strategies were effective in delivering an equitable recovery, and for whom? 

We’ve built this Pandemic relief and recovery portfolio of 40+ impact evaluations, descriptive studies,studies and resources to begin to answer thisat question. This page features links to resultsevaluations, briefs on key takeaways, and additional materialmore spanning a vast array of policy areas such aslike housing, mental health, and small businesses.  These learnings can be used for policymaking and implementation to improve outcomes for all during times of crisis and beyond.

## Evaluations
We’ve partnered with federal agencies to conduct 30+ studies and evaluations to better understandon what worked to increase awareness of, access to, and equitablefair allocation of recovery programs. The resultsse evaluations offer a wealth of insights, such as like what worked to connect thousands more callers in mental health crises to a counselor, how one state saved tens of thousands of dollars by shifting away from a strategy that was ineffective, and which technique made it easier, faster and more likely for people facing housing instability to access rental assistance.
